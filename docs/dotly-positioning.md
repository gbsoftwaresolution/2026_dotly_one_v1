# Dotly Positioning

## One-Sentence Positioning

Dotly is the premium alternative to exchanging phone numbers: a polished, permission-based way to share who you are and how people should reach you.

## Product Strategy

- Win high-intent connection moments where phone-number exchange feels too blunt, too personal, or too low-context.
- Position Dotly as a better first exchange for modern professionals, operators, creators, and communities who care about presentation, trust, and control.
- Make the product feel like an upgrade, not a utility: more intentional than a contact card, safer than giving out a number, and more personal than a link-in-bio.

## Target Users

- Professionals who meet new people often and want a cleaner first impression
- Founders, operators, sales teams, recruiters, and community builders
- Creators and hosts who want discoverability without giving away direct personal access
- Privacy-conscious users who want to control when and how contact details are revealed

## Why It Is Better Than a Phone Number

- A phone number is raw access; Dotly is controlled access.
- A phone number carries no context; Dotly shows identity, intent, and relevance.
- A phone number is static; Dotly is a living profile that can evolve without resending details.
- A phone number feels transactional; Dotly feels premium, modern, and deliberate.
- A phone number is easy to overshare; Dotly supports approval-based sharing and safer boundaries.

## UX Principle

Sharing should feel effortless in the moment, premium in presentation, and controlled after the exchange.

## Product Pillars

- Premium first impression: every share should feel more thoughtful and elevated than handing over a number.
- Permission by default: users should decide what gets shared, with whom, and when.
- Context-rich identity: profiles, personas, and relationship signals should make every connection more meaningful.
- Ongoing control: users should be able to update, manage, and protect their contact surface over time.

## Messaging Do/Don't

### Do

- Say Dotly is a premium, modern, or smarter way to connect
- Emphasize control, trust, presentation, and intentional sharing
- Frame the product as better for first exchanges, networking, events, and new relationships
- Use language that feels confident, polished, and human

### Don't

- Describe Dotly as just a digital business card or contact app
- Lead with technical features before the user benefit
- Make the product sound casual, disposable, or purely social
- Compare against phone numbers only on convenience; the stronger frame is quality plus control

## Key CTA Language

- Share your Dotly
- Connect without giving your number
- Make a better first exchange
- Share the right way to reach you
- Connect with context

## Team Reminder

When writing copy or designing flows, treat the phone number as the old default and Dotly as the premium upgrade.
